Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 17, 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to MarkFlow ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how your information is collected, used, and safeguarded when you use our web-based markdown editor application.

MarkFlow is designed to be a client-side application. We prioritize your privacy by keeping your data local to your device whenever possible.

2. Information We Collect

We believe in data minimization. We only collect the information necessary to provide our services.

  • Google OAuth Data: When you connect your Google account to use Gemini AI, we receive your email address, name, and profile picture. This data is processed via Google Identity Services and stored locally on your browser.
  • API Keys: If you manually enter API keys for services like OpenAI or Anthropic, these are stored securely in your browser's Local Storage. We never transmit these keys to our own servers.
  • Local Content: The markdown documents you create are stored in your browser using IndexedDB/Local Storage. They are not uploaded to any central server unless you explicitly use a cloud-sync feature (if available).

3. How We Use Your Information

The limited information we collect is used solely for the following purposes:

  • To authenticate your requests to third-party AI providers (e.g., passing your OAuth token to Google's Gemini API).
  • To personalize your user interface (e.g., displaying your profile picture and name).
  • To save your application preferences and documents locally on your device.

4. Third-Party Services

MarkFlow integrates with third-party Artificial Intelligence providers. When you use the AI features, the content of your editor (the prompt and relevant context) is sent directly to these third-party APIs.

Please review the privacy policies of the providers you choose to use:

  • Google Privacy Policy (for Gemini features)
  • OpenAI Privacy Policy
  • Anthropic Privacy Policy

5. Data Security & Storage

Your documents, settings, and authentication tokens are stored locally on your device within your web browser. We do not maintain a backend database containing your personal information or documents. If you clear your browser data, your MarkFlow data will be deleted.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We encourage you to review this page periodically for the latest information on our privacy practices.

7. Contact Us

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us by creating an issue on our GitHub repository.
